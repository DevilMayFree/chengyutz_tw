Corporate ESG (The ESG content provided is based on the 2021 version, and the 2022 edition is expected to be completed and updated in December.)

PlayNitride Inc. continues to deepen research and development in the field of MicroLED with a new and pioneering point of view, providing the most appropriate products in the MircoLED application field. With the expansion of operations and the expectations of stakeholders, PlayNitride Inc. launches the Sustainability Management (ESG) promotion plan, examines the company’s operating environments and risks, and at the same time, establishes a sound corporate governance and integrity management culture, and effectively protects the rights and interests of various stakeholders to demonstrate corporate value.  The vision and objectives of the company are established by integrating with the business strategy of sustainability management.

Environmental Sustainability

Carbon Emission Management

In accordance with the ISO14064-1:2018 standard in 2022, we will conduct the quantification, supervision, reporting and confirmation or verification of greenhouse gas inventories or plans to disclose the clarity and consistency of the greenhouse gas inventory, and at the same time handle internal employee education and training courses, assist in the implementation of significant indirect emission analysis and related emission coefficient construction, compile the activity data of each base, perform internal verification operations, and write the company’s GHG inventory procedure documents, emission inventories and inventory reports.

Supply chain management

1. Management of Hazardous Substances

Suppliers are required to provide the “Commitment not to Use Prohibited or Restricted Substances in Environmental Regulations” and Safety Data Sheet (SDS) of products, parts and materials, etc., and the third notary unit test data report to confirm compliance with the RoHS/REACH product hazardous substance standard requirements.  We conduct regular audits of qualified suppliers to confirm environmental protection and the maintenance of the product hazardous substance management system.

2. Conflict Minerals Management

The products, parts and materials required of our suppliers do not contain conflict minerals (gold (Au), tantalum (Ta), tin (Sn), tungsten (W), cobalt (Co) and other minerals and derivatives originating in the Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC) and its surrounding countries and regions.  Suppliers are required to complete and submit the content to PlayNitride Inc. in accordance with the format of the Conflict Minerals Report Template (CMRT).

Waste Management

The company follows the requirements of waste cleaning regulations, implements waste management, and obtains ISO14001 certification, and continues to operate according to the management system.  In addition to the approval of the competent authority, the relevant business also declares the quantity of waste output, storage, removal and treatment by means of the network in accordance with the relevant regulations on waste clean-up and the norms of the Environmental Protection Bureau, and also entrusts qualified manufacturers recognized by the EPD to remove and dispose of the waste according to the treatment method described in the waste clean-up plan.   In the future, the company will continue to work towards process waste reduction, resource conservation, decrease in waste generation, material recycling and reuse, and relief in environmental load, and achieve the goal of waste reduction and resource recycling and reuse.

Environmental Safety and Health Education and Training

In order to promote the safety and health management of activities, products, service processes and relevant stakeholders related to operations, the company establishes, implements and maintains the procedures and standards of the management system that meets the requirements of the ISO 45001 international standard, effectively manages any negative impact on safety and health or anything that does not comply with the regulations, and continuously improves safety and health performance.   

According to the requirements of the management system and regulations, the implementation of occupational safety and health education and training for each new employee, and the regular implementation of retraining, emergency response education and training, and relevant provisions for all employees, to deepen the concept of safety of colleagues.   The above is also included in the annual education and training plan for continuous implementation.

Internal Audit

1. Purpose of Internal Audit 

The design, implementation and internal inspection of the Company’s internal control system shall be handled in accordance with the guidelines for the establishment of the internal control system and related laws and regulations of the public offering company. 

2. Internal Audit Organization

The Company has set up an internal audit unit, which is subordinate to the Board of Directors to work exclusively for internal audit work.  In accordance with the company’s operating scale, business situation, management needs and other relevant laws and regulations, the Audit Supervisor shall be assigned full time with a suitable and appropriate number of people to which he belongs.  The appointment and removal of the Head of Internal Audit shall be subject to the consent of the Audit Committee and a resolution of the Board of Directors.  The qualifications of internal auditors meet the statutory requirements for appointment and continue to study for the required number of hours.

3. Operation of Internal Audit

The internal auditors perform their duties in a spirit of transcendent independence and with an objective and impartial standpoint, with due professional attention.  The Audit Supervisor regularly reports to the Audit Committee on audit operations and attends the Board of Directors as an observer.  The Company’s internal audit business is based on the results of the risk assessment to prepare an annual audit plan, specifying the audit items, time, procedures and methods.  Auditors conduct regular or irregular routine and project audits, and audit results are submitted to the Audit Committee with working papers and related materials to ensure that the Company’s internal control system is continuously and effectively implemented.  

All units and subsidiaries within the Company are urged to regularly assess the effectiveness of the internal control system on a regular basis every year.   The internal control self-assessment should be performed by the head of each unit according to the actual operation content of the department, the internal control system design and implementation effectiveness assessment.  And then the internal audit unit reviews the self-assessment report of each unit and its subsidiaries, and the internal control deficiencies and abnormal matters found by the audit unit, as the main basis for the board of directors and the general manager to evaluate the effectiveness of the overall internal control system and issue an internal control system statement.   Internal auditors shall disclose the deficiencies and abnormalities in the internal control system discovered in the internal audit operations, listed in the internal control system declaration, and self-assessment and certified accountant project review, and follow up after the report is audited, and make a tracking report on a quarterly basis to determine that the relevant units have taken appropriate improvement measures in a timely manner and listed as an important item in the performance appraisal of various departments.   After the audit report and the tracking report are audited, they shall be submitted to the Audit Committee for review before the end of the month following the completion of the audit project.   If the internal auditor discovers a major violation or the company is in danger of being seriously damaged, it shall immediately make a report and notify the board of directors and the Audit Committee.
